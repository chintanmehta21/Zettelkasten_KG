## h1: What ethical and legal tiers does the zero-day vulnerability market operate across, and what types of buyers participate in each tier?

**Answer:** The zero-day vulnerability market operates across three broad ethical and legal tiers: white, gray, and black [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"].

The **white tier** involves legal bug bounties, where companies typically offer rewards for the discovery of vulnerabilities [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. The **gray tier** encompasses unregulated government acquisition of zero-days [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. For example, a US hacker selling to the NSA would be considered part of this tier [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. The **black tier** is characterized by criminal use [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. An example of this would be a US hacker selling to Russia's Operation Zero [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. Geopolitical factors can blur these distinctions, as selling to Operation Zero might be gray for a Russian hacker, while selling to the NSA via Zerodium would be black for them [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"].

## h2: What role do brokers like Zerodium play in the zero-day ecosystem, and how do prices vary across exploit types?

## h3: How does the existence of a covert zero-day market shape both nation-state cyber warfare and law-enforcement investigations?

**Answer:** The covert zero-day market is a complex ecosystem that enables both sophisticated cyber warfare and critical law enforcement efforts [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"].

For nation-state cyber warfare, these unpatched software flaws are highly valued and traded by various actors, including nation-states [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. Examples include Stuxnet, which used four zero-days to disrupt an Iranian nuclear facility, and NotPetya, which used one zero-day to paralyze Ukraine [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. Zero-days were also reportedly used by the Saudi government to track journalist Jamal Khashoggi, and "Operation Triangulation" involved a chain of four zero-days to infect iPhones via iMessage, granting full control [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. The market is categorized into gray (unregulated government acquisition) and black (criminal use) tiers, with geopolitical factors blurring these distinctions [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"].

Despite their dangers, zero-days are crucial tools for law enforcement agencies [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. In early 2024, an 11-country task force used a likely zero-day in PHP to dismantle the infrastructure of the LockBit ransomware gang, which had controlled nearly half of the global ransomware market [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"]. This demonstrates the dual-use nature of zero-day exploits, enabling both harm and protection [id="yt-zero-day-market-covert-exploits"].
